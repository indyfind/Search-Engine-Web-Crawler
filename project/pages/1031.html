    Pokemon Sun and Moon Exclusives and Version Differences

    Edit Page    Last Edit: December 7, 2016 - 1 day 6 hours ago

    This section of IGN's wiki guide and Pokemon Walkthrough documents exclusive Pokemon Sun and Moon Version Differences, such as Version Exclusive Pokemon, Legendary Pokemon and other distinctions between the two retail versions of the next-gen Pokemon.

    EditVersion Exclusive Pokemon

    As it is with all other Pokemon titles, certain Pokemon will only appear in one version of the game and not the other. This includes the very rare Solgaleo and Lunala, the legendaries of the games that play a key role to the story.

    EditTime Difference

    Pokemon Sun and Moon will run 12 hours apart from each other. If you're playing Sun, the game will run in real time in accordance with your 3DS clock. If you're playing on Moon, the game will shift 12 hours from the 3DS clock.

    This applies to almost the entire game, save for specific story scenes that take place at certain times of the day, no matter the game. So why is this important? There will be a few Pokemon the either only appear at either day or night, or their chances of appearing will differ depending on the time.

    EditTotem Pokemon

    Some of the Totem Pokemon in Pokemon Sun and Moon will change depending on the version. For example, the Totem Pokemon encountered in Verdant Cavern on Melemele Island will be Gumshoos in Pokemon Sun, but the nocturnal Alolan Raticate in Pokemon Moon.
