      I felt the EXACT same way about 1984, I thought I was the only one honestly. Winston was such a very dry character but I understand he was made to enhance the mundane world around him, but man halfway through I was waiting for the climax to hit and even when it actually did I felt like I knew what was going to happen. Out of all of the books I read this summer it was my most disappointing one. I read Brave New World and 1984 right after one another and I felt myself comparing the too almost always throughout my read. Brave New World was more enjoyable, and the characters actually felt more charismatic than Winston did, I recommend that book.

      I personally liked Mother Night the most, even though on goodreads Breakfast of Champions gets more praise. Mother Night, to me, just was Kurt at his finest. I really enjoy satirical writing and Kurt is the best of the best IMO. I recently bought Lamb by Christopher Moore. Apparently he's like a modern day Vonnegut, so after I read NOS4A2 I will be most likely reading that. Agatha Christie's book was not too bad its really a quick read, I read it on vacation. Its supposed to be her best work, I haven't read anything else by here so I can't compare. If you are looking for something quick and are into the whole mystery horror type of thing I think its a decent read.

      All and all I can't recommend Mother Night enough. Breakfast of Champions is solid too, but Mother Night was quite enjoyable, I almost finished it in one sitting.
       
      teaakayy, Jun 23, 2016
