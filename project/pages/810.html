      Everything You Need to Know About Final Fantasy XV

      Share.
      Ten years is a VERY long time.
      By Zachary Ryan

      Now that the release Final Fantasy XV is (finally) here, it’s the perfect opportunity to do a little time traveling and look back at all the highs, lows and in betweens of FFXV’s long and often tumultuous development. The following is a crash course of all the biggest news and updates.

      In The Beginning

      Final Fantasy VX began its notorious ten year development cycle as a Final Fantasy XIII side story. Dubbed “Final Fantasy Versus XIII” and announced alongside of FFXIII at E3 2006, the game was lead by the team behind Kingdom Hearts II and sported a more action-oriented style.

      Versus Trailer
      04:38
      Trailer

      The same trailer made an appearance at Tokyo Game Show 2006, and we gleaned precious little information from then Director Tetsuya Nomura in early 2007. Over the next few years, news of Final Fantasy Versus XIII began to slow to a crawl. We’d get the occasional trailer, or scant details from Nomura and his team. By the end of 2010, talk of Final Fantasy Versus XIII had gone nearly silent and we didn’t get a major update for almost three years.

      The Big Reveal

      It wasn’t until February 2013 that rumors started circulating that Final Fantasy Versus XIII had jumped to the Playstation 4 and was perhaps getting renamed and promoted to… Final Fantasy XV.

      News: Next Final Fantasy Versus XIII Reveal Date "is Set"
      01:21
      IGN NEWS

      The big reveal came at E3 2013. Final Fantasy Versus XIII was officially re-revealed as Final Fantasy XV.

      Final Fantasy XV E3 2013 Trailer - E3 2013 Sony Conference
      03:35

      We also got our first, lengthy, look at FFXV’s sword-warping, wall-crawling gameplay:

      Final Fantasy XV Battle Gameplay First Look - E3 2013

      We want to hear it.

      Oh also, apparently the game was almost a musical…?

      After the big announcement at E3, Nomura addressed the re-branding and why the title change needed to happen. In early 2014, we learned that the game was apparently “quite far into development”.

      News: Final Fantasy XV 'Quite Far Into Development'
      00:56
      Got feedback on our player?
      We want to hear it.

      As if Square Enix were making up for lost time, this news marked the beginning of an onslaught of information, trailers and events that would lead up to the release of Final Fantasy XV.

      Information Overload

      Tokyo Game Show 2014 brought a deluge of new FFXV info. First and foremost was a brand new trailer.

      Tetsuya Nomura announced that he would be leaving Final Fantasy XV in the capable hands of Final Fantasy Type-0’s Hajime Tabata while he took over work on Kingdom Hearts III. Furthermore, we learned that the game was 55% complete and  that fans would get a FFXV demo packed in with Type-0 HD.

      Final Fantasy XV: Episode Duscae Gameplay Trailer
      01:20
      Got feedback on our player?
      We want to hear it.

      As 2014 was winding down the FFXV team walked fans through their upcoming demo (albeit, in Japanese) and Tabata reflected on the importance of honoring the history of the series.

      After the release of this tone-piece trailer, Tabata-san stressed the importance of the series’ move in a more realistic direction.

      At PAX 2015, We got to go toe-to-toe with one of the series’ signature enemies, the Behemoth, in the impressive and lengthy demo, Episode Duscae.

      In the midst of all the hype, a bit of controversy popped up around Final Fantasy XV’s all male party members.
