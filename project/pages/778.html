      PlayStation VR: Everything You Need to Know

      Share.
      It's been a long road getting from there to here.
      By Jared Petty

      PlayStation VR is almost upon us! How better to prepare yourself for the imminent launch than taking a look at the announcements, teasers, reveals, and reviews that brought us to this moment? Below is a compilation of the most important PSVR news, coverage, and game info released over the past three years, following PlayStation VR's evolution from rumor to the Project Morpheus reveal and into the consumer-ready headset we reviewed this week.

      Here's everything you need to know about PSVR.

      The Rumor Breaks

      PlayStation VR's first appearance in the mainstream came just after Gamescom 2013 with a report that Sony had initially planned to reveal a VR headset at the prominent European trade show, but pulled the presentation at the last minute.

      Sony Allegedly Working on PS4 VR Headset
      01:09
      IGN NEWS

      Earliest reports indicated an internal Driveclub VR integration had been in development within Sony. A month later, patent filings were unearthed revealing possible design specifications for a Sony virtual-reality unit.

      Originally filed in May 2013, the head-mounted display patent images included pictures of headset and camera integration which proved remarkably similar in appearance to PlayStation VR's final commercial iteration.

      PS4 VR Headset Reportedly Already in Devs' Hands
      01:00
      IGN NEWS

      GDC Reveal

      By February 2014 reports had emerged that Sony planned a GDC 2014 reveal for their new virtual reality hardware. In early March a story emerged that the new hardware was already in developers' hands, but there was still no official acknowledgement of PlayStation virtual reality hardware by Sony.

      Sony Reveals Project Morpheus PlayStation 4 VR Headset
      01:54
      Got feedback on our player?
      We want to hear it.

      Sony formally announced Project Morpheus at GDC 2014, showing off a VR headset designed for integration with PlayStation 4 hardware. The March 18 announcement revealed an early prototype of the PSVR, including the characteristic white and black coloration and blue glowing accent lighting that endures in the final product.

      At the presentation, PlayStation head Shuhei Yoshida talked about the possible revolutionary applications for gaming, and Dr. Richard Marks of PlayStation R&D discussed the possible non-gaming applications. Marks also talked about the integration of the PlayStation Camera, Move, and PS4 controller into the overall design.

      Project Morpheus: GDC 2014 Presentation
      58:13
      Got feedback on our player?
      We want to hear it.

      Epic Games and Crytek were both named as interested developers, and Thief and EVE: Valkyrie were both announced as compatible titles. A demo called The Deep by Sony London was demonstrated. Initial hardware specs included low latency, 360 degree tracking, and a 1080p display.

      First Impressions

      IGN's hands-on experience with the Morpheus prototype left editor Scott Lowe with a favorable impression, drawing positive comparisons to the development-era Oculus Rift. The Morpheus' visual fidelity was praised, though we noted the field of view was narrower than what we'd experienced with Oculus.

      PlayStation Project Morpheus VR Headset Editors' Impressions
      05:14
      Got feedback on our player?
      We want to hear it.

      The graphical and immersive qualities of The Deep demo impressed us, and we also spent some time in EVE: Valkyrie, which allowed for some more direct comparisons to the Rift hardware. We noticed more motion blurring in Morpheus than with the Rift 2 dev kit. We also noted the comfort of the prototype PSVR headset.

      The Story Grows

      Following the big reveal, the spring was filled with emerging stories on Morpheus. Ubisoft's Lionel Raynaud stated that "VR would need to sell at least 1 million units to be viable for development." Sony Online Entertainment expressed interest in VR integration for Everquest Next.

      SOE 'Hugely Interested' in PS4 Virtual Reality Headset For EverQuest Next
      00:56
      IGN NEWS

      In April, Sony's Guy Longworth revealed that Facebooks Mark Zuckerberg tried out Sony's VR hardware before purchasing Oculus, and it was revealed that PlayStation VR support would be retroactively added to Surgeon Simulator.

      Facebook to Acquire Oculus VR for $2 Billion
      00:47
      Got feedback on our player?
      We want to hear it.

      New Impressions

      IGN's Marty Sliva spent some time with PSVR during New York Comic Con 2014. Marty was supremely impressed with his time playing The Castle demo, which combined Morpheus with Move controller utilization, and playing Street Luge, a speedy racing experience.

      The Deep, Project Morpheus Demo - Direct Feed Gameplay
      04:16
      Got feedback on our player?
      We want to hear it.

      In early 2015, IGN's Alex Roth also spent time demoing Morpheus, and reported on his excitement about the immersive power of gunplay in The Heist.

      VR Transformed Me into a FPS Badass...and a Horse
      03:51
      Got feedback on our player?
      We want to hear it.

      Pricing and Details

      The news and previews continued through the spring and summer of 2015 and into E3 season. In March, Shuhei Yoshida commented that PSVR games would likely appear at E3, and that Sony intended to keep the price as low as possible.

      Project Morpheus Made Us Believers in VR
      19:12
      Got feedback on our player?
      We want to hear it.

      Early reports in May indicated that the Morpheus would see a release before June 2016. Also in May, Sony revealed that a Morpheus-centric first party studio, North West Studios, was forming in England.

      At E3 2015, Sony had plenty of VR demos available, including early builds of Superhypercube, The Heist, Harmonix Music VR, and more.
