    Weekly Theme Blog: Most Memorable Character Deaths

    November 2, 2015 by Saleem44

    Note: Though, there are no names mentioned in sub-headers, this blog will contain major spoilers.  Please, read carefully.

    Character deaths is one of the main reason shows like The Walking Dead and Game of Thrones are so popular.  The unexpected fate of one of the most lovable - or hateable - characters can shock the viewer, which is great and memorable if it's executed right.

    These are my top ten most memorable character deaths in video games.  Like I mentioned above, this will contain spoilers, so I advice skipping entries of games you haven't played yet if you wish to remain spoiler free.

    Batman: Arkham City

    The Dark Knight has been fighting The Joker for as long as I remember, so you don't need me to tell you how shocked I and millions of others were at the ending of Batman: Arkham City.  I was puzzled and confused.  How could this happen?  I mean, he's The Joker!  He can't die!  But at the end, he was indeed dead.  It was a bold move from Rocksteady, to kill the prince of crime himself and one of the most popular villains in pop culture.

    I haven't played Arkham Knight yet, but it's hard imagining it without Harley's Mr. J.

    BioShock

    Andrew Ryan.  He was quite the character, wasn't he?  An arrogant man like that is not so easily forgotten.  The one and only moment you come face to face with him ended up in him being whacked to death.  What's so memorable about it?  He orders you to kill him.  That's pretty messed up.  To make things even more interesting, the protagonist is actually his illegitimate son. 0_O

    Final Fantasy VII

    One of the early - if not the first - shocking deaths was Aerith's in Final Fantasy VII.  Her death by the end of the first disc took us all by surprise as Sephiroth's sword pierced right through her chest.  It turned out to be one of the most memorable and most iconic moments as it picked at number one on IGN's Top 100 Video Game Moments.

    Mass Effect 3

    I had no idea that I could save both The Quarians and The Geth when I first playrd the game.  Though, Tali is one of my favorite video game characters, I took The Geth's side.  After she witnessed her whole race being wiped out, she committed suicide by jumping off a ledge.  I couldn't shake the feeling of guilt for days.

    Metal Gear Solid 3: Snake Eater

    The characters in Metal Gear Solid 3: Snake Eater are the main reason why I love this game so much.  The Boss, though, is the standout.  A mentor to the Legendary Big Boss, and the woman he truly loved.  After battle of the decade, The Boss was relieved of her pain by her former disciple in a field of white flowers.  There is no way it can get any more memorable than that.

    Persona 3

    Persona 3 is a special game.  It had some great and truly emotional moments, but to me personally, the protagonist's death tops them all.  It wasn't the first time the protagonist died in a game, but I find it to be the most memorable.  It feels weird saying it, but it was a beautiful moment.  Laying on Aigis' lap while she thanks you for giving her a meaning to life is so damn emotional.  God damn you, Atlus!  I love you so much.

    Red Dead Redemption

    Rockstar are known for creating amazing characters, and with Red Dead Redemption, they created one of the best in John Marston.  The former outlaw's death was unexpected, at least for me.  I seriously thought they might go with a happy ending.  But poor John got gunned down instead by God knows how many people.  His death is definitely one of the most memorable out there.  Kudos to Rockstar for writing such an amazing character in an amazing game.

    The Darkness

    It's always tough to watch someone - even if it's just a bunch of pixels - get their brains blown out up close.  That's what happened to Jenny, Jackie's girlfriend.  Paulie shot her right in front of Jackie, who was powerless and couldn't do anything to prevent it.  What makes it even more heartbreaking is that she made him a chocolate cake a few hours ago for his 21st birthday.  What kind of a monster of an uncle kills your girlfriend on your freaking birthday!?  Poor Jenny didn't deserve this.

    The Last of Us

    The Last of Us punched us in the guts as early as 20 minutes into the game with Sarah's death.  An innocent girl who sold hardcore drugs to buy a watch for her father's birthday deserves to be awarded daughter of the year.  That was far from it, though, as she was shot and died in the arms of her crying father.  That was brutal from Naughty Dog.  For the 20 minutes she's been in the game, she was able to get me to the point of tears by the end if it.

    The Walking Dead

    There is no denying that TellTale's The Walking Dead was an emotional game, and there was little doubt about what was going to happen next when Lee got bitten by a walker.  His last moments were as emotional as it gets as he asks Clementine to kill him, so he couldn't come back as a walker and endanger her.  The fact that Clementine had to be the one to kill Lee made things much more depressing and memorable.

    What's your most memorable character deaths?

    Thanks for reading.
