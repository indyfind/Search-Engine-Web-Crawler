    Top Video Game Spies

    November 6, 2015 by ChromaticSparks

    A new James Bond film is here! While it doesn't reach the introspective and dramatic heights of Skyfall, Bond's latest outing, Spectre, is still a fun spy romp. Bond himself embodies all the classic (or clichéd, depending on who you ask) elements of a spy. He's dashing, a ladies man, charming, witty, sneaky, strong, drives cool cars, and is dedicated to uncovering the truth at any cost. He may be the most famous spy in the history of fiction. Hold on...I'm pretty sure being a famous spy makes you terrible at the job but I digress. Let's talk about the most famous video game spies shall we?

    Joanna Dark - Perfect Dark

    Joanna Dark blasted her way onto the scene in 2000's Perfect Dark for the N64. A skilled marksman, tactician, and hand-to-hand combatant, Joanna works for the Carrington Institute. She performed so well on her initial tests at the Institute that she was given the codename "Perfect Dark". You may have noticed something about that name...it's freaking rad and makes her sound like the sneakiest sneak master ever. Oh, and I guess it's also the name of the series. Anyways, Joanna has made a living trying to stop conspiracies and evil corporations from taking over the world and doing generally nasty stuff. In terms of spy gear Joanna has access to a fairly broad arsenal. Using flying camera drones, hacking devices, scanners and even laptop guns, means she is well equipped for any situation. Joanna Dark is above all a professional, and that's why she deserves a spot on the list.

    Big Boss - Metal Gear/Metal Gear Solid

    There are some soldiers that have become so legendary on the battlefield that the mere mention of their name strikes fear into their enemies' hearts. Big Boss is one of these legends. Originally codenamed Snake, he was mentored by the original Boss. The Boss was a fierce woman who developed the art of Close Quarters Combat or CQC, and taught everything she knew to Snake. After he surpassed the Boss he was given the title Big Boss. He eventually went on to create multiple freelance mercenary companies like the MSF and Diamond Dogs. He also joined a few secret organizations that came close to ruling the world through a military economy. Big Boss was considered by many to be a perfect soldier, he was unmatched in CQC, an excellent marksman, and his stealth capabilities were unrivaled. Unlike the other entries on this list Big Boss became kind of a jerk. He helped create deadly shadow organizations and sought to control the world in a misguided attempt to fulfill the Boss' dying wish. Even though he eventually became a villain, Big Boss was one of the most influential spies in both the Metal Gear universe and gaming as a whole.

    Leliana - Dragon Age

    Leliana differs from the other people on this list for a couple reasons. First, while she is capable of sneaking and stabbing with the best of them, she's more of a spymaster than an actual sneakster. Second, she isn't a modern spy in a jumpsuit with fancy tech. Leliana works in the Middle Age fantasy world of Dragon Age, meaning she doesn't have the same tools and gadgets as these other spies. She more than makes up for her lack of technology with her skill, guile and networks of agents. Leliana's power has a long reach. She is capable of gathering information, influencing politics, or assassinating targets anywhere within Thedas. Being able to take targets down with a bow or a pair of daggers means she's no slouch in combat either. Her skills and knowledge have been a massive boon to her allies, and a severe detriment to her enemies.

    Sam Fisher - Splinter Cell

    Sam Fisher is the most classic of spies. He wears a skin tight black sneaky suit and night vision goggles, and he even has the requisite gruff and gritty spy voice. Working for the Third Echelon branch of the NSA, Fisher has carried out the blackest of black ops missions all over the world. Aiding him in his adventures are his sneaking skills which he developed while working for the CIA and the Navy SEALs. Fisher is a tough dude, capable of dealing and taking a considerable amount of punishment in battle. His training also allows him to infiltrate almost any organization, group or compound undetected. He's extremely capable in the arts of deception, having spent considerable time undercover with various criminal groups around the world. Fisher is kind of like James Bond, if Bond favored subtlety over showmanship.

    What do you think makes a great video game spy? Did I miss anyone you have on your list? Let me know in the comments below, and as always, thanks for reading!
