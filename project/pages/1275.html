      The one about Kanye was cool, but nothing special and not something you're likely to revisit. As for him kinda calling Kanye out on his bullshit, I don't really care for it. I respect that as a fan of Kanye and just as a person he is as entitled to his opinion on Kanye and his situation as anyone else, I just don't care about that situation and don't even feel like it's worth Cole talking about. And then there's the reaction of the people. People reacted like Cole said something guys like Taxstone, Charlemagne and TI haven't been saying for a while about Kanye. Only reason nobody has come at him/come out about it on a track is that I don't think it's that important to anyone.

      Second track about "Lil whatever" rappers is another cool one. I think its a little hypocritical that the same people (and it's definitely the same people) that are praising Cole for calling them out, will turn right around and embrace those same rappers. The song itself is actually pretty good, Cole showing growth on that one, new flows and such.

      All that being said I think the most interesting thing happening right now is the perception of Cole I feel like Coles supposed depth as an artist is overstated, and I feel like he's somewhat overrated as a rapper compared to his peers. When Cole gets "deep" its actually pretty damn shallow. He covers some more serious topics but in a superficial way that never moves me. And I think that's where he shows his lack of ability as an artist compared to say KRIT or Kendrick.

      For the longest time I didn't even really want to like Kendrick, but when I sit down and listen to GKMC and TPAB and Section 80 I am moved. When I listen to Cole I hear the words and can relate to a lot of what he's saying but dude never moves me like that.

      I'll definitely check this new album, and these new singles were pretty good but I definitely wasn't blown away by them like a lot of other people were.
