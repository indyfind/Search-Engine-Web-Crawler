    Season 6 Differences

    Edit Page    Last Edit: June 3, 2016 - 6 months 8 days ago

    This page contains a log of TV/book differences and discrepancies in Season 6 to A Feast for Crows (fourth novel) and A Dance with Dragons (fifth novel). The content on these pages contain spoilers.

    EditCharacter Changes

    • Davos Seaworth is at The Wall when Jon Snow is killed. In the books, he is in White Harbor at that time.
    • The series shows the aftermath of Miranda's death. This character does not exist in the books.
    • Stannis Baratheon is dead (or at least believed to be) in Season 6 of the TV series. He is still alive in the books.
    • Sansa Stark and Theon Greyjoy are on the run from Ramsay Bolton in the first episode of Season 6. In the books though, Sansa is unmarried and living in the Eyrie, while Theon is a prisoner of Stannis Baratheon.
    • Brienne rescues Sansa and Theon from Bolton soldiers near Winterfell. In the books, Brienne is not anywhere near the north. She is instead with Jaime Lannister in the riverlands.
    • Myrcella Baratheon is dead in the show, but still alive in the books.
    • Margaery Tyrell is still in the custody of the Faith at the start of Season 6. At this point in the books, she has been allowed to stay with Randyll Tarly until the day of her trial.
    • Areo Hotah, Trystane Martell, and Doran Martell are killed in the first episode of Season 6. They are all still alive in the books.
    • Tyrion Lannister and Varys are serving as Meereen's temporary rulers. In the books, neither of them are in the city. Tyrion is outside, in the midst of the "Second Siege of Meereen."
    • Varys meanwhile is in King's Landing.
    • Jorah Mormont and Daario Naharis are hunting for Daenerys Targaryen's whereabouts. In the books, both of them are outside of the city. As such, neither travels to Vaes Dothrak.
    • Jorah Mormont does not have Greyscale in the books.
    • Arya Stark's blindness occurs much sooner in the books than in the TV series. The circumstances behind it, and how she gets her sight back, are also different. 
    • Melisandre is never revealed to actually be an old woman in the books.
    • Euron Greyjoy has mismatched colored eyes in the books (one blue and one black), and wears an eye-patch to conceal the black one. Euron also has pale blue lips, as he regularly drinks shade of the evening.
    • Jaime Lannister is not in King's Landing at this point in the novels, and does not interact with the High Sparrow. Instead, he ignores Cersei's request to return to the city to defend her in the trial of combat. Jaime was last seen on the road to Rivverun.
    • Coldhands is not revealed to be Benjen Stark in the books. Additionally, he is described as being much more corpse like in appearance.
    • Coldhands is also introduced much earlier, and accompanies Bran to the three-eyed raven (three-eyed crow in the books).


    • Arya Stark's story in Braavos plays out much differently in the books. Instead of being tasked with assassinating an actress, Arya actually becomes an performer herself in the troupe. She has not yet left the Faceless Men, or assumed her old identity. 

    EditScene Changes

    • Daenerys  is not recognized by her Dothraki captor, Khal Moro. In the books however, The khalasar that finds her is instead led by Jhaqo, one of Drogo's former lieutenants.
    • In the books, Arya Stark's blindness training is completed after she uses warg-like abilities to see through the eyes of a cat.
    • Arya has not yet left the Faceless Men in the books. 
    • The flashback to the Tower of Joy in the HBO series implies that Bran believed his father defeated Ser Arthur Dayne single handedly. In the books however, Ned Stark explicitly states that Dayne would have killed him had it not been for Howland Reed's intervention. 
    • The kingsmoot plays out very differently in the books. In that version, each candidate is required to present a symbolic gift to the Ironborn, and make a speech. Additionally, there were six candidates.
    • In the books, Asha Greyjoy (Yara in the HBO series) comes very close to winning at the kingsmoot.
    • Theon Greyjoy is not present at the kingsmoot.
    • Euron never admits to killing Balon Greyjoy in the books.
    • In the novels, Euron is victorious at the kingsmoot after presenting a bounty of gold and treasure, as well as a horn he claims can control dragons.
    • Brienne and Sansa do not travel together to Castle Black in the books. Brienne was last seen in the riverlands, and it is unlikely that she has reunited with Sansa, who is currently in the Vale.

    EditAdded or Extended Scenes

    • The HBO series depicts the aftermath of Jon Snow's death. This has not yet happened in the books. 
    • It also depicts the aftermath of the Battle of Winterfell, an event that has not yet occurred in the books.
    • Accordingly, all of the scenes that take place in the north are original to the HBO series.
    • The series shows Jaime Lannister's return from Dorne. This story arc did not occur in the books, as Jaime never traveled to Dorne.
    • All of the Season 6 scenes in Dorne so far are original to the HBO series.
    • All of the Season 6 scenes in Meereen so far are original to the HBO series.
    • Balon Greyjoy's death is only alluded to in the books, and is believed to be accidental (albeit suspicious). In the HBO series, it is explicitly shown to be a murder. 
    • Theon and Yara Greyjoy do not steal a fleet in the books. In that version of the story, both Theon and Yara are captured (separately) by Stannis Baratheon's forces after the events of the kingsmoot.
    • Daenerys never travels to Vaes Dothrak in the books.
    • The Children of the Forest are not revealed to be the creators of the White Walkers in the books. No events from that era in history are ever explicitly detailed.
