    Dishonored 2 Wiki Guide

    Edit Page    Last Edit: November 4, 2016 - 1 month 4 days ago
     
    Released Platforms

    November 11, 2016.

    		PC
    PlayStation 4
    Xbox One
    Developer Publisher
    Arkane Studios Bethesda
    Official Sites
    Official Site // On Facebook // On Twitter

    Dishonored 2 is an upcoming action/-adventure, first person stealth game coming from french developer Arkane Studios. It's a sequel to the original Dishonored, which released back in 2012. Dishonored was officially announced on June 14, 2015 during Bethesda's first E3 conference.

    A Collector's Edition for the game was announced at E3 2016 that includes Corvo's mask, Emily's ring, a metal game case, poster, in-game content, and a digital version of the game.

    EditStory

    Dishonored 2 follows the story of the original Dishonored, but changes up the narrative by allowing players to take control of the protagonist from the first game, Corvo Attano and a familiar character Emily Kaldwin. Emily is one of the main characters from the first game, where her kidnapping plays a crucial part in the narrative and influences the actions of Corvo in the game.

     

    As shown in the cinematic trailer from Bethesda's E3 2015 conference, Emily is now an Assassin taking up her own cause. Corvo is also seen dawning the iconic mask that he wore in the original game. The game is set in a city unlike Dunwall that's being ruled by tyranny and in the midst of revolt.

    EditSetting

    Unlike the original Dishonored, Dishonored 2 is set in a city on the southern edge of the world called Karnaca. The city is similar to the setting of the original game of victorian aged buildings, however it has been shown to feature more plant life and is surrounded by forests and mountainous terrian.

     

    Dishonored 2 features a single campaign, but dialogue options will differ depending on which lead character you choose. Corvo and Emily have different sets of powers and can engage stages in different ways. There is no co-op mode and players will not be able to switch between characters once they have made their initial choice of role. 

    EditGameplay

    The E3 trailer showed a lot of old gameplay elements from the original Dishonored, such as the ability to bend time and blink. Emily was also using a new item to the series, an organic blue grappling hook, that allowed her to scale buildings. It has been confirmed that you can play the entire game without killing anyone, similar to the original Dishonored.

     

    Emily's abilities will differ from Corvo's, giving Emily the power to affect her world in different ways. Among her unique powers is one that allows her to see glimpses of the past in a given situation. Other abilities include using Far Reach to pull yourself rapidly across a distance.

    Two new powers were revealed. Domino allows you to link two enemies together through an invisible force, knocking them towards each other when they try to walk away. Shadow Walk allows you to squeeze through tight spaces to quickly maneuver your way to your enemy.
